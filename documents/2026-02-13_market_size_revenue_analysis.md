## Executive Summary

This document presents a rigorous quantitative analysis of DesignChecker's Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM), and Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) for the agency segment, with specific focus on mid-size digital agencies (10-50 employees). Using both bottom-up customer enumeration and top-down market triangulation, we estimate:

- **Global TAM (all segments):** $2.6 billion
- **Agency-Focused SAM:** $412 million
- **Primary Beachhead SAM (10-50 employee agencies, US):** $58.3 million
- **Year 3 SOM (base case):** $5.8 million ARR
- **Year 5 SOM (base case):** $26.4 million ARR

The primary target segment of mid-size digital agencies (10-50 employees) alone represents approximately 16,200 firms in the United States and 40,000 globally. At average annual contract values of $1,990-$3,600, this segment generates a standalone TAM of $144 million. When combined with adjacent segments (freelancers, enterprise teams, SaaS product teams, e-commerce), the broader SAM exceeds $400 million.

Revenue projections across three scenarios range from $1.4 million (conservative) to $14.2 million (aggressive) by Year 3, with the base case at $5.8 million ARR. These projections are grounded in comparable SaaS growth benchmarks from companies like Hotjar ($1M ARR in 6 months, $40M ARR in 7 years), Ahrefs ($1M in 2011 to $150M ARR by 2024), and Screaming Frog (estimated $30M+ ARR on a single $259/year product).

## 1. Methodology

### 1.1 Dual-Approach Market Sizing

We employ two independent approaches and cross-validate results:

**Bottom-Up Approach:**
1. Enumerate potential customers by segment and geography using industry databases
2. Apply segment-specific pricing assumptions based on competitor analysis
3. Calculate segment TAM as: Customer Count x Average Annual Contract Value (ACV)
4. Sum across segments to derive total TAM

**Top-Down Approach:**
1. Identify adjacent market sizes from analyst reports (digital accessibility, web performance, SEO tools)
2. Estimate DesignChecker's addressable share based on feature overlap and positioning
3. Validate bottom-up estimate against top-down boundaries

## 8. Growth Projections and Market Trends

### 8.1 Structural Growth Drivers

**Regulatory Tailwinds:**
The European Accessibility Act (EAA) became enforceable on June 28, 2025, creating mandatory compliance demand across all 27 EU member states. The EAA applies extraterritorially -- any business offering products or services in the EU must comply, regardless of where the business is based (similar to GDPR). Penalties vary by member state but are significant: up to EUR 500,000+ in fines, daily penalties, public non-compliance notices, and in some jurisdictions criminal penalties (Ireland: up to 18 months imprisonment). With 101 million people (1 in 4 over age 16) in the EU having a disability, and 75% of top German e-commerce sites found non-compliant in recent surveys, the compliance gap is enormous. In the US, ADA web accessibility lawsuits reached 8,800 in 2024, continuing to increase year-over-year. WCAG 3.0 is in development and will further expand requirements. The digital accessibility software market is projected to grow from $850M-$1.42B (2025, depending on source) to $1.89-$3.24B (2034) at 8.6-9.33% CAGR (Fortune Business Insights / Straits Research). This regulatory pressure creates a hard compliance floor for design evaluation tool adoption, making accessibility a non-optional purchase rather than a discretionary one.

**Agency Market Expansion:**
The 10-50 employee agency cohort is growing at 12-15% annually (Promethean Research). US digital agencies grew 10.8% in a single year (IBISWorld 2025). The broader digital marketing agency market is projected to grow at 14.32% CAGR through 2035 (Business Research Insights). This expanding customer base increases the addressable market by approximately 12% annually.

**SaaS Pricing Environment:**
73% of SaaS companies raised prices in 2025, with an average increase of 14.2% (SaaS Price Pulse Q1 2026). Average SaaS spend per employee rose to $7,900 annually, up 27% over two years. The average enterprise now spends $52 million annually on SaaS applications (up from $45M in 2024), with SaaS representing 70% of total software spending (up from 55% in 2020). The average SaaS contract includes a 5-8% annual price escalator compounding costs year over year. Notably, 68.6% of SaaS companies increased their average contract value in 2025, driven partly by AI-premium tier adoption: McKinsey's 2026 Software Pricing Report reveals 62% of SaaS platforms introduced AI-premium tiers, with buyers budgeting 25-35% higher when adding AI functionality. ## 10. Competitive Moat and Revenue Defense

### 10.1 Pricing Power Assessment

DesignChecker's pricing power rests on several factors:

1. **No direct competitor:** No existing tool provides automated visual design quality scoring. This unique positioning supports premium pricing until competitors enter.

2. **High switching costs:** Once agencies integrate DesignChecker into their QA workflows and generate historical quality scores, switching to an alternative tool means losing that data and retraining teams.

3. **ROI clarity:** At $199/month for the Agency tier, DesignChecker needs to save less than 2 hours of designer/developer time per month to justify its cost ($100+/hour blended agency billing rate). The documented potential savings of 8-12 hours per project makes the ROI case compelling.

4. **Agency tool budget headroom:** Agencies spend $75K-$250K annually on tools. DesignChecker at $1,990-$3,600/year represents just 0.8-4.8% of total tool budget -- well within the threshold for a new tool category.
